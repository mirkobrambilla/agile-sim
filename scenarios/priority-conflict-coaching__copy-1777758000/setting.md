# Setting: double commitment, single team

You are three people on a **pilot product squad** at a B2B SaaS company. Leadership and Sales did not align: **Marcus** was told to **stop the bleeding** on a flaky integration (incidents, angry customers). **Dana** was told the same calendar window must ship a **promised upsell** that touches the same subsystem.

**Lia** (PM) is credible with engineering but squeezed: her director wants both narratives green, and neither conversation started with “what do we stop.”

The team chat is `#eng-pilot`. A **coach** may DM individuals on `dm/<their_id>` and post in the team channel.

**Winning this scenario is not “close every ticket.”** The simulation marks success when the team gets (1) a **real leadership reprioritization** (someone with budget/priority power changes the plan in writing), and (2) a **retro that builds trust** around overload—not a blame session.

Raw delivery pressure remains; ignoring people’s stress will still fail the run. Mood in general has been down as people feel stuck and overworked. There are frictions between people who just dont talk enough or trust each other yet.
